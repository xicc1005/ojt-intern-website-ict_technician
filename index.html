COMPUTER LABORATORY POLICY & GUIDELINES

1. Do not loiter or socialize in the computer laboratory. Computer laboratories are to maintain a "library like" atmosphere.

2. Do not leave your personal belongings unattended. Laboratory assistant and in-charge are not responsible for lost, stolen or moved items. It is your responsibility to keep your own property safe and secure.

3. The viewing of pornographic or other sexual explicit materials in the laboratory is not permitted.

4. The user is responsible for observing all copyright and licensing laws.

5. Absolutely no alcohol, tobacco or other drugs permitted in the laboratory. Anyone suspected to be under the influence of drugs or alcohol will be asked to leave and possibly lose their laboratory privileges.

6. The laboratory assistant or computer technician will assist in using the laboratory facility.

7. One student with computer laboratory subject(sm) is allowed to use the computer units.

8. Since the workstations are deepfreeze to prevent virus attack on relevant files thus, students are required to use flash drive for file storage.

9. The course instructor is the only authorized person who can manipulate and access the server. He or she is accountable for any lost computing devices or peripherals during his or her scheduled time of duty. Thus no student is permitted to use the computer units without the presence of their course instructor.

10. The course instructor should make sure that the computer units and circuit breakers are properly turned off, AVR cables are unplug, windows and doors are closed, chairs and so with the computer peripherals are arranged before leaving the laboratory.

11. In order to provide productive and professional environment for all users, disruptive behavior, game playing or viewing of inappropriate materials will not be tolerated.

12. Headphones may be connected to any computer units, provided that the volume be kept at an appropriate level.

13. Users are not permitted to interfere with the operation of any laboratory printer. This includes removing pages while the printer is processing and expelling finished documents. This is to ensure that the pages for any user's print job are not shuffled and misplaced. Additionally, all printer control consolers are off limits. The laboratory assistant or in-charge should be contracted for errors that may occur.

14. Do not attempt to repair the monitor and CPU. SDSSU Administration advises repair by authorized computer laboratory technicians only.

15. Position keyboards and mouse to prevent bumping or dropping them off the work surface.

SAFETY AND PRECAUTIONARY MEASURES

Computer and Internet Laboratories

1. Establishment of perimeters for computer laboratories based on the school's rules. These include blocking prohibited websites and estab- lishing criteria for firewall.

2. Posting of "Computer Lab Rules" that clearly states computer laboratory restrictions. These may include prohibition of food and drink, download ing software, opening attachments, removal of equipment, access to illic it sites and more.

3. Plugging all computer equipment into a surge protector to prevent dam- ages in case of lightning storms.

4. Setting-up weekly updates or automatic updates for computers.

5. Installation of anti-spyware program on computers and/or network.

6. Back up of relevant files on a regular basis.

7. Use of hard disc clean up and defragmentation utilities regularly.

8. Not to unplug printers, scanners and other connected machines when the computers are on.

9. Turning off all computers by selecting the shutdown option on the desk- top.

10. Cleaning of computer laboratory regularly.
